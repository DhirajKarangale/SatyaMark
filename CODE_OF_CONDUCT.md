# Code of Conduct

SatyaMark is an open-source verification infrastructure.  
We are committed to maintaining a respectful, inclusive, and professional community for everyone involved.

This Code of Conduct outlines expectations for all contributors, maintainers, and community participants.

---

## Our Commitment

We strive to provide a safe and welcoming environment for all, regardless of:
- Experience level
- Background or nationality
- Identity or personal characteristics
- Technical opinions or viewpoints

Participation in the SatyaMark community should be constructive, collaborative, and grounded in mutual respect.

---

## Expected Behavior

Positive contributions include:
- Communicating respectfully and professionally
- Being considerate of different perspectives
- Providing constructive feedback
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and correcting them
- Focusing discussions on improving the project and its ecosystem

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:
- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation of any kind
- Personal attacks, insults, or inflammatory comments
- Publishing private information without consent
- Disruptive behavior that hinders collaboration
- Any conduct that is inappropriate in a professional open-source setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Project documentation and communication channels
- Any space where SatyaMark is officially represented
